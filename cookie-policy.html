Cookie Policy

Last Updated: November 13, 2025

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide a better user experience by remembering your preferences, maintaining your session, and analyzing site usage.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Keep you logged in to your account
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analyze how you use our platform
  • Improve our services and user experience
  • Ensure security and prevent fraud

3. Types of Cookies We Use

4. How to Manage Cookies

4.1 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies

4.2 Our Cookie Preferences

You can manage optional cookies through our cookie preferences center (available in Settings):

  • Essential Cookies: Always enabled (required for service)
  • Functional Cookies: Can be disabled (will lose personalization)
  • Analytics Cookies: Can be disabled (helps us improve the service)

Note: Disabling essential cookies will prevent you from using key features like logging in and saving progress. Disabling functional cookies will reset your preferences on each visit.

5. Do Not Track (DNT)

We respect Do Not Track (DNT) browser settings. When DNT is enabled, we:

  • Disable analytics cookies
  • Minimize data collection to essential functions only
  • Do not track your browsing activity

6. Cookie Duration

Our cookies have the following lifespans:

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain for a set period (up to 1 year)
  • Performance Cookies: Typically 1 year
